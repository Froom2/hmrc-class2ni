Paying your Class 2 National Insurance for self-employment

You have to pay Class 2 National Insurance when in self-employment. Class 2 contributions are Â£2.75 a week for the tax year 2014 to 2015 and are paid as part of your Self-Assessment.

Use this service to:

  • view the periods where you have to pay Class 2 National Insurance
  • change your dates if any are incorrect
  • understand how much Class 2 National Insurance you have to pay
  • recalculate how much you have to pay if you change or delete any periods

The online service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

What you need to know

  • any gaps between dates may be because you claimed a benefit where you got Employment and Support Allowance (ESA), were working abroad or you stopped and re-started self-employment
  • you still have to pay Class 2 National Insurance during a temporary break in self-employment if you intend to continue with self-employment
  • if you choose to delete a period where you have to pay Class 2 National Insurance, it will be permanently deleted and can't be recovered

You can make changes outside of the relevant tax year.
