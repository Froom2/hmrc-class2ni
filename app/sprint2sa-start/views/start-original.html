Paying your Class 2 National Insurance for self-employment

You have to pay Class 2 National Insurance when in self-employment. Class 2 contributions are Â£2.80 a week for the tax year 2015 to 2016 and are paid as part of your Self Assessment.

Use this service to:

  • view your self-employment dates
  • change your self-employment dates if any are incorrect
  • understand how much Class 2 National Insurance you have to pay
  • recalculate how much you have to pay if you change or delete any self-employment dates

The online service is also available in Welsh (Cymraeg).

What you need to know

  • any gaps between dates may be because you claimed a benefit where you got National Insurance credits or you stopped and re-started self-employment
  • you still have to pay Class 2 National Insurance during a temporary break in self-employment if there is intention to continue with self-employed work
  • if you choose to remove a self-employment period, it will be permanently deleted and can't be recovered

You can make changes outside of the relevant tax year.
